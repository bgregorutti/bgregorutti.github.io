**Safety Line** is a French company providing data-driven solutions for aviation efficiency and safety to airlines and airports worldwide. I spent **9 years** there (2011–2020), growing from ML Scientist to Head of Data Science.

---

## Head of Data Science · 2017–2020

I led a team of **5 data scientists and engineers** in a start-up environment, splitting my time roughly 50/50 between management and hands-on technical work.

### Projects

**Aircraft fuel consumption optimisation**
Systematic reduction of fuel burn across airline fleets, using flight data recorder (FDR) streams — up to **1,000 parameters recorded per second**. Clients included **10–20 airlines** across Europe and beyond.

**Airport analytics & emissions reporting**
Processing of ground radar and airport operations data to improve efficiency and safety. Partnership with **Aéroports de Paris (ADP)**.

**European R&D project — Thales**
Collaborative research on aviation data within a European-funded project involving **Thales** as industrial partner.

### PhD Supervision

Co-supervised a PhD student with **INRIA** on dynamical system identification and optimal control (2015–2018). Co-author of **4 peer-reviewed articles** published during this period, plus **1 patent**.

### Key Achievement

Oversaw the design and delivery of a **fully automated ML pipeline** — from raw flight data decoding to Dockerized production deployment — enabling systematic fleet-wide analysis at scale.

## Machine Learning Scientist · 2015–2017

Large-scale flight data processing and ML algorithm design for aircraft fuel consumption reduction.

**Key Achievement**

Designed and implemented ML algorithms and Python APIs for **climb phase fuel optimisation** — delivering up to **10% fuel savings per flight**. The system was deployed in production for multiple airline clients.

---

## Research Scientist · 2011–2015

Initial research work on statistical methods for flight data analysis, variable selection, and functional data — forming the basis of my **PhD (2011–2014)** at Université Pierre et Marie Curie.

Topics: random forests, variable importance, functional data clustering, signal processing for FDR data.
